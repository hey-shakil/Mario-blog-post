Quick Answer

The original Super Mario Bros. is a platforming video game released by Nintendo in 1985 for the NES. Created by Shigeru Miyamoto, it popularised the side-scrolling platformer genre, rescued the video game industry from the 1983 crash, and established Mario as a global pop-culture icon.

The Origins: How a Plumber Saved an Industry

Before Super Mario Bros., the video game market was still reeling from the infamous 1983 crash. Consoles were viewed as a dying fad. Nintendo needed a system-seller for their new Famicom â€” and visionary designer Shigeru Miyamoto delivered.

Mario had previously appeared as "Jumpman" in the 1981 arcade game Donkey Kong and the 1983 arcade hit Mario Bros. However, the 1985 console release took the character out of a single fixed screen and into a massive, scrolling world. By introducing a smooth scrolling camera, vibrant colours, and physics-based momentum jumping, Nintendo created a blueprint that every subsequent 2D game would borrow from.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the storyline of the original Super Mario Bros.?
Mario and his brother Luigi traverse the Mushroom Kingdom to rescue Princess Toadstool (later named Peach) from the villainous Bowser, who has turned the kingdom's inhabitants into blocks and Goombas using dark magic.
Why is Mario a plumber?
Shigeru Miyamoto originally made Mario a carpenter in Donkey Kong because the game took place on a construction site. When creating Mario Bros. (1983), the setting featured underground pipes, so his profession was changed to plumber to fit the environment.
What is the Minus World in Super Mario Bros.?
The "Minus World" is a famous glitch level. By performing a specific wall-clipping jump at the end of World 1-2, players can bypass the warp zone and enter World -1 â€” an endless underwater level that players cannot escape without dying.
How many worlds are in the original Super Mario Bros.?
There are 8 distinct worlds, each containing 4 sub-levels, bringing the total to 32 standard levels. The 4th level of every world is a castle where you must face a decoy or the real Bowser.
Can you still play the 1985 Super Mario Bros. today?
Yes! You can play it via the Nintendo Switch Online NES library, on retro mini-consoles like the NES Classic Edition, or by finding original hardware and cartridges at retro gaming stores.
